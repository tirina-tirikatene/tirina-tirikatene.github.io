Te Whare Tapa Wha

Taha tinana (Physical wellbeing)

Activities:

  • Walk my 2 younger children to their pre-school x2 days a week
  • The days I do not walk my children to preschool, still ensuring I am going for a walk
  • Set an alarm x3 times a week at a resonable hour and ensure I go to bed
  • Have a fresh fruit/veg smoothie every day for morning tea

Taha whanau (Family wellbeing)

Activities:

  • Walking with my younger 2 children x2 mornings a week to preschool instead of driving
  • Making time once a fortnight for my partner and I to have dinner out together
  • Ensuring I am taking my children to visit their great-grandparents once a fortnight
  • Movie night every Friday night with my older children. This connects to my own childhood which is the best for re-living those memories as a child with my whanau

Taha wairua (Spiritual wellbeing)

Actiities:

  • Start journaling - write in it each night before bed
  • Practice more positive self-talk/affirmations each morning with my children

Taha hinengaro (Mental and emotional wellbeing)

Activities:

  • Each Sunday write down a plan for the week ahead to lessen the stress of remebering everything and to know whats on
  • Ensuring I am making concious efforts to spend time with each of my tamariki to lessen the mind guilt, even just 5 minutes to read a book
  • Stick to the positive affimations
  • Ask for help when needed to lessen the mental load and stress

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

Activities:

  • While walking my tamariki to preschool, take time to slow down. Show them some bugs or pick some flowers, enjoy the fresh air with them
  • When having my morning coffee, open the door or sit outside to feel the freshness and hear the birds talking
