Reflection

Utilising your journal findings and the high-level overview of the Human Skills learnings you have covered in Foundations - answer each of the following questions:

• What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

I have learnt a lot about the way I learn and cope with stress. I’ve learnt that I struggle with not needing to understand the ‘why’ I am very much always someone that asks why and find it helps sit in my head better when I know the reason ‘why’. I’ve learnt to laugh at myself when I’m trying something for the 5th time and its still not working. I have always been one to sit inside my comfort zone and not push myself out of it too far, this course has been the start of a large push very much outside the walls of my comfort zone so handling that hasn’t always been easy, it’s been great to test my determination and perseverance. I’ve learnt that I need to be kinder to myself and praise myself for the small wins that come along which really does give me a wee confidence boost by doing so.

• What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I’d say empathy plays a big part as it can help connection. From my own learning journey I would say having empathy for someone else and yourself can help grow as a team. Understand everybody’s journey is different can help support learning and working in a team environment. Self awareness can impact your own mind and how you take things on board. I think it can be a positive thing in learning and programming as you understand your own boundaries, weaknesses and strengths. Being confident in taking on criticism knowing its to help your own growth.

• What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

I think the way it has resonated with me in my recent life events. Reading, learning and understanding how much being in the right frame of mind and how important a growth mindset is plus what it can do. I’m quite surprised by how much of it I can implement in my home life around my children also.

• What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

The hardest parts of it are actually putting it into practise. Taking it from the screen and popping that into the mind and implementing it in daily life. When things get tough or don’t go the way they should, remembering the things that were learnt.

• Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think so much time is put into it because its important. It’s important as it isn’t very common to know/learn these things at any other point in life. I also think its important because during this course when things get hard, human skills will be important to know and utilize. It gives everyone a base for falling back onto when needed during this learning journey.

• Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, I don’t think it has been a waste of time at all. Over the years I have put a lot of time into my mental health and think this has been a great booster for that. It’s always good to acknowledge healthy habits and healthy ways to keep your mental and physical health on track. It becomes hard when doing something that isn’t the ‘norm’ and learning how to manage stress, emotions and your way of thinking has been great. I think the amount of time put into this has had a positive outcome for myself in the way of re-thinking when I am feeling less than positive about myself and the work I had been putting in. It was nice to automatically go into a frame of mind where I was thinking, ‘it’s okay, try again. Take a break, try again. Ask for help. Its okay to ask for help. You’ve got this.” That is literally my thought process each time I’ve had an issue so far.
