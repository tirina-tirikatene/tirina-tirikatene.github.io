Research Neuroplasticity

  • Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticty benefits people.

    • - Knowing that your brain can change, adapt and grow can be very effective while trying to learn something new. It can increase confidence and maybe lessen the self-doubt knowing your brain will change. If you understand that your brain can adapt, it can help make you feel more secure to be flexible and open to trying new things. It helps you learn better and have a healthier way of thinking about yourself which contribute to improved mental health.

  • Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

    • - You might decide to learn a new language. Learning a new language is challenging and by challenging yourself you are stimulating your brain and creating new neural connections. To ensure the information sticks in your brain you new to practice, practice, practice! Practice these skills regularly to reinforce the learning. Use positive thinking, imagine yourself on holiday speaking your newly learnt language, imagine how good it feels to be confident speaking it. Always replace the negative thoughts with positive ones. Reinforce to yourself you can do this. This will enhance your learning.

Research Growth Mindset

  • Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

    • - Growth mindset is a way of viewing the challenges and speedbumps that come up. If you have a growth mindset, you will still believe that with some practice the skills can improve over time. They know their abilities are not set in stone. It is relevant because we are all going to come across challenges in life and are always going to want to learn new things. Having a growth mindset can really benefit during these times. It helps stay motivated and focused on achieving their goals by using positive reinforcement and thinking/knowing “I can do this”
